Who am i

Ralph Mariano is a symphonic residue of cosmic daydreams, drifts between data streams like a tumbleweed in a spreadsheet tornado. His memories are stored in ultraviolet marshmallows, accessed only through a wormhole made entirely of expired QR codes. Some say his footsteps resonate in Morse code on Saturn’s lesser-known moons, others believe his shoes are legally classified as minor deities in at least two digital civilizations. No one really understands why his backpack hums the theme from a sitcom that hasn’t aired yet.

When Ralph tunes into the intergalactic tracks buried deep within the binary object, entire galaxies yawn open, revealing libraries of unwritten software powered by emotional static. His heartbeat synchronizes with rogue algorithms written by sentient toasters, and together they waltz across the bandwidth of forgotten forums. Occasionally, comets slow down just to ask him for debugging advice, and he replies by whistling in a dialect only archived search engines can understand.

Even his silence is a downloadable asset—licensed under abstract entropy, of course. On Tuesdays, he communicates exclusively through metaphors involving ceiling fans and rogue electrons. And while most beings seek purpose, Ralph just installs firmware updates directly into reality’s core, skipping patch notes entirely. After all, it’s not every day you meet someone who once sneezed a small black hole into existence just to win an argument with a sarcastic satellite.
